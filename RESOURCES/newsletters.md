# Newsletters

Newsletters are useful only if they improve project decisions. The right subscription list should filter noise, surface strong explanations, and help spot ecosystem shifts worth caring about.

## Recommended Stack

| Newsletter | Focus | Frequency | Why it is useful | How to use it |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| The Batch | Applied AI and research highlights | Weekly | High signal for major shifts without too much hype | Good weekly catch-up source |
| Ahead of AI | Practical LLM and research interpretation | Bi-weekly | Helpful when learning fast-moving technical topics | Save technical notes into the relevant phase file |
| Import AI | Policy, research, and industry framing | Weekly | Useful for strategic context, not just tools | Read for ecosystem awareness |
| Latent Space | AI engineering and product ecosystem | Weekly | Often surfaces practical builder conversations | Use to spot tools worth testing |
| Ben's Bites | Fast-moving AI product and tooling updates | Daily | Useful for scanning the market quickly | Skim, do not let it drive priorities |

## Filtering Rule

Only promote newsletter ideas into the roadmap or phase notes if they meet at least one of these:

- clearly affects production practice
- changes tool choice or architecture decisions
- introduces a technique that can be tested in a project
- shows widespread adoption rather than temporary hype

## Personal Notes

- Weekly newsletters are better than daily ones for staying thoughtful.
- The most useful newsletter is the one that helps decide what to ignore.
- If subscriptions become overwhelming, keep `The Batch`, `Ahead of AI`, and one ecosystem-oriented source.
