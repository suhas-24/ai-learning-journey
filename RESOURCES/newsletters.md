# Newsletters

Newsletters should help me notice what matters, not keep me in a state of perpetual intake. The best ones clarify current practice, show what is changing in the ecosystem, and give me ideas worth testing in a project.

## How To Use This List

- read weekly, not reactively
- save useful ideas into the right phase file
- ignore anything that does not affect a build decision

## Recommended Stack

### The Batch

Focus: applied AI and research highlights.

### What it teaches

- the shape of the current AI landscape
- broad shifts in models, tools, and product usage

### Best use

- weekly overview during Phases 2 to 11

### Why it matters

- it is a good signal source for what is changing at a high level

### Ahead of AI

Focus: technical interpretation of research and practical model behavior.

### What it teaches

- how to read model and research developments without overhyping them
- the connection between ideas and engineering impact

### Best use

- Phases 2, 5, and 10

### Import AI

Focus: policy, research, and industry framing.

### What it teaches

- the broader ecosystem beyond tools
- why some shifts matter strategically

### Best use

- Phases 2 and 8

### Latent Space

Focus: AI engineering and product ecosystem.

### What it teaches

- practical builder conversations
- tool adoption patterns
- the state of the ecosystem from a builder perspective

### Best use

- Phases 4, 7, and 11

### Ben's Bites

Focus: fast-moving AI product and tooling updates.

### What it teaches

- what is launching
- what builders are experimenting with
- what might be worth a closer look

### Best use

- Phase 2 for scanning, not for deep study

## Filtering Rule

Only promote a newsletter idea into the roadmap or phase notes if it does at least one of these:

- changes production practice
- changes a tool choice or architecture decision
- introduces a technique I can test in a project
- appears to have real adoption rather than short-lived hype

## Personal Notes

- Weekly newsletters are easier to use well than daily ones.
- The most useful newsletter is the one that helps me decide what to ignore.
- If the list starts feeling noisy, keep the highest-signal ones and drop the rest without guilt.
