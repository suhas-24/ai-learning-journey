# Systems Thinkers

This guide focuses on people who repeatedly explain AI work as a systems problem: tradeoffs, reliability, deployment, feedback loops, and governance.

If you are still building your vocabulary, `systems thinking` just means looking at how the pieces affect each other instead of judging one piece in isolation.

## How To Learn From This Category

- Ask how each person frames the whole system, not just one feature.
- Notice how they talk about tradeoffs like cost, latency, and reliability.
- Use them when you need help seeing the big picture.

## Chip Huyen

### What to learn from her

Chip Huyen connects models, data, evaluation, and product requirements into one engineering story. Her work is especially useful when you need to stop thinking of AI as prompt craft and start thinking of it as system design.

### Core themes and subtopics

- AI engineering as a discipline.
- Evaluation loops and data feedback.
- Reliability, latency, and cost tradeoffs.
- Deployment and iteration under real constraints.

### Best phases

- Phases 2, 7, 9, and 11.

### Watch for

- She is especially helpful when you need to explain how a product improves over time, not just how it launches.

## Dario Amodei

### What to learn from him

Dario Amodei is useful for understanding capability growth, safety framing, and the strategic consequences of stronger models. He is less about implementation details and more about why safety and capability conversations matter.

### Core themes and subtopics

- Capability scaling.
- Risk framing and safety concerns.
- Long-term impact of advanced systems.

### Best phases

- Phase 8 when you need broader context for guardrails and risk.

### Watch for

- This is a context source. It helps you understand why certain safeguards matter.

## ByteByteGo

### What to learn from this source

ByteByteGo is useful when you need clean explanations of architecture, distributed systems, and operational tradeoffs. It helps AI builders avoid treating AI systems as separate from normal software engineering.

### Core themes and subtopics

- Architecture tradeoffs.
- System boundaries and scaling.
- Reliability and maintainability patterns.

### Best phases

- Phases 7 and 11.

### Watch for

- This source is especially useful when you want a clear systems diagram in your head before you build.
