# Tool Builders And Practical Operators

This guide focuses on people who teach through workflows, tools, and real project decisions. They are most useful when you want to see how ideas become commands, scripts, traces, and operating habits.

These are the people to study when you want to see the difference between knowing an idea and using it in a real project.

## How To Learn From This Category

- Watch how they break a problem into steps.
- Notice what they inspect, measure, and refuse to guess.
- Use them as examples of disciplined practice, not as personalities to imitate.

## Harrison Chase

### What to learn from him

Harrison Chase is useful for understanding orchestration patterns, workflow composition, and how the LangGraph ecosystem thinks about stateful agent systems.

### Core themes and subtopics

- Agent orchestration.
- State graphs and tool use.
- Retrieval-aware workflows.
- Application-level abstractions over raw model calls.

### Best phases

- Phases 4 and 7.

### Watch for

- He is helpful when you want to see how a workflow becomes explicit instead of accidental.

## Simon Willison

### What to learn from him

Simon Willison is one of the best teachers for practical experimentation. He consistently shows how to test an idea quickly, inspect the results, and stay grounded in reproducible workflows.

### Core themes and subtopics

- Tool-centric experimentation.
- Reproducible prompts and command-line workflows.
- Careful evaluation of new model features.
- Practical use of LLMs without hype.

### Best phases

- Phases 2, 4, and 9.

### Watch for

- He is a strong model for keeping the work concrete and inspectable.

## Greg Brockman

### What to learn from him

Greg Brockman is most useful for understanding how platform capabilities evolve and how product decisions can reshape what builders should pay attention to.

### Core themes and subtopics

- Platform evolution.
- Product direction.
- Ecosystem shifts that affect builders.

### Best phases

- Phase 2 as ecosystem context.

### Watch for

- Use this source for direction-setting, not for implementation detail.

## Anthropic Engineering

### What to learn from this source

Anthropic Engineering is useful for concrete writeups on model behavior, agent patterns, and deployment decisions. It is one of the better places to see research-informed practice.

### Core themes and subtopics

- Agent design.
- Tool use patterns.
- Deployment lessons.
- Safety-informed implementation choices.

### Best phases

- Phases 2, 7, and 8.

### Watch for

- The value here is in seeing how a team explains its reasoning, not just the final pattern.
